Data-driven bioregionalization: A seascape-scale study of macrobenthic communities in the Eurasian Arctic

Dmitrii Pantiukhin, Dieter Piepenburg, Miriam L. S. Hansen, Casper Kraan

Published on 09 September 2021 | Journal of Biogeography

Read the full article

This study conducts the first model-based assessment of the biogeographical subdivision of Eurasian Arctic seas to delineate spatial distribution and boundaries of macrobenthic communities on a seascape level and to project potential shifts in distribution and composition by 2100.

Ongoing projects

Enhancing gelatinous zooplankton annotation with YOLO v8 and ByteTrack

This project addresses the challenge of detecting and counting gelatinous zooplankton in underwater video footage. YOLO v8 employed for object detection and ByteTrack for multi-object tracking. Our pipeline begins with semantic pre-annotation using GroundingDino and SAM, continues with training the YOLO model, and concludes with overlaying ByteTrack for accurate organism counting.

Deployment of the langchain agent with custom functions to Critterbase

Project Ongoing

View the project

CRITTERBASE is a continuously expanding data warehouse aimed at enabling marine ecologists worldwide to work with high-quality and high-resolution data readily. This project involves the deployment of the Langchain Pandas Agent with custom functions to enhance the database's capabilities.

Environmental DNA in an ocean of change: status, challenges and prospects

Charlotte Havermans, Annkathrin Dischereit, Dmitrii Pantiukhin, Madlen Friedrich, Ayla Murray

Published on 18 March 2022

Read the full article

Studies with environmental DNA (eDNA) have grown in the last two decades, and the application of eDNA has increased exponentially since 2010, although at a slower pace in the marine system. We present an overview of the literature on eDNA studies of marine metazoans and evaluate recent achievements in answering questions related to species distribution, biodiversity, and biomass.
